FACTS
WHAT DOES PAYPAL DO WITH YOUR PERSONAL INFORMATION?
Rev. November 27, 2024

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHY?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Financial companies like PayPal choose how they share your personal information.
Federal law gives consumers the right to limit some but not all sharing. Federal
law also requires PayPal to tell you how it collects, shares, and protects your
personal information. As a licensed money transmitter and financial services
provider, PayPal is also subject to the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA), Bank
Secrecy Act (BSA), USA PATRIOT Act, and FinCEN regulations — meaning a
significant portion of data collection is legally mandated, not just commercial.

Please read this notice carefully to understand what PayPal does.

This policy applies to: PayPal accounts, Venmo (see separate Venmo policy),
Braintree, Hyperwallet, Xoom, Honey, and other PayPal family products.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT INFORMATION DO WE COLLECT?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The types of personal information PayPal collects depend on which products and
services you use. This information can include:

  IDENTITY AND KYC (KNOW YOUR CUSTOMER) DATA:
  - Full legal name, date of birth, Social Security Number or Tax ID
    (required by federal law for identity verification and AML compliance)
  - Government-issued ID numbers and documentation
  - Home address history

  FINANCIAL DATA:
  - Bank account numbers, debit and credit card numbers (stored via tokenization)
  - Transaction history: purchases, sales, transfers, and refunds
  - Linked account balances and payment method details
  - PayPal Credit / Buy Now Pay Later data (if applicable)
  - Products, preferences, sizes, and styles inferred from purchase history
    (Note: Starting Summer 2025, this data may be shared with partner merchants
    for personalized shopping — users are opted in by default and must
    manually opt out via Settings > Data & Privacy > Personalized Shopping)

  CONTACT AND COMMUNICATION DATA:
  - Email address, phone number, and mailing address
  - Communication preferences and customer service interaction records
  - Marketing consent status

  DEVICE AND TECHNICAL DATA:
  - IP addresses and device fingerprinting (hardware characteristics,
    browser configuration, operating system version)
  - Cookie data and session identifiers
  - Login patterns: frequency, time-of-day, and geographic location of access

  THIRD-PARTY FINANCIAL AND FRAUD DATA:
  - Credit bureau information (Experian, TransUnion, Equifax) for credit products
  - Fraud signal data from financial intelligence networks and early warning
    services
  - Merchant data about transactions processed through PayPal
  - Publicly available information about businesses (for merchant accounts)

  BEHAVIORAL DATA:
  - Feature usage patterns and product interaction data
  - Shopping behavior inferred from payment patterns across PayPal-enabled
    merchants (used for fraud detection, risk scoring, and — beginning 2025 —
    personalized shopping recommendations)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
HOW DOES PAYPAL USE THIS INFORMATION?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PayPal uses collected information for the following purposes:

  - Process transactions and maintain your account(s)
  - Verify your identity and comply with federal financial regulations
    (BSA, AML, KYC, FinCEN reporting obligations)
  - Detect and prevent fraud, unauthorized access, and financial crime
  - Respond to court orders, legal investigations, and regulatory requirements
  - Report to credit bureaus and consumer reporting agencies (where applicable)
  - Communicate with you about your account, transactions, and services
  - Market PayPal products and services to you (can opt out)
  - Personalized shopping recommendations to partner merchants (Beginning 2025;
    opt out via Settings > Data & Privacy > Personalized Shopping)
  - Develop and improve PayPal products and services

HOW DOES PAYPAL PROTECT YOUR INFORMATION?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Security measures that comply with federal financial law (GLBA)
  - Computer safeguards: firewalls, encryption (256-bit SSL), tokenization of
    payment card data to PCI DSS standards
  - Secured physical facilities and access controls at data centers
  - Fraud monitoring systems and real-time transaction risk scoring
  - Data governance program with retention periods based on legal and regulatory
    requirements
  - Privacy Impact Assessment process for new data uses

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
HOW LONG IS YOUR INFORMATION RETAINED?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PayPal retains personal information based on legal, regulatory, and business
requirements:

  - Transaction records: Up to 7 years (required by BSA/AML regulations)
  - Account data: Retained while account is active; deleted after a reasonable
    period post-closure subject to legal holds
  - Marketing data: Retained until you opt out or close your account
  - Fraud and security data: Retained as required for investigations and law

  IMPORTANT: The 7-year financial record retention requirement means that
  deletion rights are significantly limited for PayPal compared to non-financial
  services. Users cannot fully delete financial transaction records during the
  mandatory retention window even upon account closure.

DEFINITIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Affiliates:      Companies related by common ownership or control, including
                   PayPal Credit, PayPal Debit, Venmo, Braintree, Hyperwallet,
                   Xoom, Honey, and other PayPal Holdings subsidiaries.
  Nonaffiliates:   Companies not related by common ownership or control,
                   including partner merchants, service providers, financial
                   institutions, credit bureaus, and fraud detection networks.
  Joint Marketing: A formal agreement between nonaffiliated financial companies
                   that together market financial products or services to you
                   (e.g., co-branded cards with banks or insurance partners).
  KYC:             Know Your Customer — identity verification requirements
                   mandated by federal anti-money-laundering regulations.

OTHER IMPORTANT INFORMATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  California Residents (CCPA/CPRA):
    California residents are NOT automatically opted in to the personalized
    shopping data sharing that begins in 2025. You must affirmatively consent.
    You have rights to know, correct, delete (subject to financial retention
    exceptions), and limit the use of sensitive personal information. Submit
    requests at: paypal.com/privacy-center | Response within 45 days.

  Virginia Residents (VCDPA):
    You have rights to access, correct, delete (subject to financial retention
    exceptions), and port your data, and to opt out of targeted advertising.
    Submit requests via paypal.com/privacy-center.

  Vermont Residents:
    Vermont residents will not be automatically opted in to the personalized
    shopping data sharing. Affirmative consent is required. PayPal will not
    disclose your personal information, financial information, or credit
    information to nonaffiliated third parties to market to you, other than as
    permitted by Vermont law.

  North Dakota Residents:
    North Dakota residents are NOT automatically opted in to the personalized
    shopping data sharing that begins in 2025. Affirmative consent required.

  Nevada Residents:
    You may call 1-888-221-1161 or visit paypal.com to be placed on PayPal's
    Do Not Call list. For more information, contact the Nevada Bureau of Consumer
    Protection: 555 E. Washington Ave., Suite 3900, Las Vegas, NV 89101 |
    1-702-486-3132 | BCPINFO@ag.state.nv.us.

  EU/EEA/UK Residents (GDPR/UK GDPR):
    PayPal relies on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) for international data
    transfers. Lawful basis for financial processing is contractual necessity and
    legal obligation. You have rights to access, erasure (subject to financial
    retention), portability, restriction, and objection. Contact:
    dpo@paypal.com or your national Data Protection Authority.
